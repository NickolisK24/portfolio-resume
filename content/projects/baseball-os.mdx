## Problem

Bullpen management is one of the highest-leverage decisions a coaching
staff makes, and the signal is scattered across pitch counts, back-to-back
appearances, warm-up usage, and recent workload — none of which live in
one place. I wanted a tool that collapsed that noise into a single,
defensible fatigue score you could act on.

## Architecture

Three modules backed by a shared ingestion layer:

1. **Ingestion** — pulls the MLB Stats API on a schedule, normalizes
   events into a Postgres schema that survives API shape changes.
2. **Fatigue engine** — weighted composite model over recent appearances,
   pitch counts, and rest days. Output is a score plus a risk tier.
3. **UX layer** — React + Tailwind front end with a bullpen board, a
   prospects view, and per-player drilldowns.

## Key engineering decisions

- **Flask over FastAPI** for this one — I wanted the simplest possible
  backend while the model was still in flux.
- **Risk tiers, not just scores** — coaches don't want a raw number, they
  want "green / yellow / red" with the reason.
- **Model weights are config, not code** — tuning doesn't require a
  deploy.

## Status

Deployed and iterating. Next up: pitch-mix-aware fatigue and a prospect
